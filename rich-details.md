### "Stalemate"
**Timeline:** Week 3 of marriage  
**Setting:** Penthouse living room, late evening

Rufus asks when Eua will stop treating him like an adversary. She immediately reverses it: "You first." Points out he's been truthful but not open—everything calculated, never vulnerable. He admits: "I wouldn't know where to start." She echoes: "Neither would I." Before leaving, she distinguishes: trusts he won't hurt her, but trusting him with who she actually is? "Two different things. Not ready. Maybe not ever."

**Key Development:**
- Their marriage is mutual standoff built on physical safety trust without emotional vulnerability
- Both honest about impasse rather than pretending it doesn't exist
- Eua distinguishes between two types of trust clearly
- Neither willing to be first to lower defenses

### "What Remains"
**Timeline:** Weeks after Stalemate, early marriage period  
**Setting:** Penthouse study, afternoon

Eua finds hidden drawer of Shinra documents, discovers her father's complete employee file (26 years, adoption papers, medical retirement). Breaks down crying while reading it, completely unguarded for first time with Rufus watching. Describes her father: loud, terrible puns, called Vesta "his Vesuvius," talked about her future knowing it likely wasn't possible as Shinra asset. Died week before Meteor thinking she was trapped. Statement while holding file: "If you want to know why I didn't become the next Sephiroth—it's because of this man and his wife." Rufus: "Any father who loved you that much would be relieved you survived. The rest is commentary." Her uncertain: "Maybe."

**Key Development:**
- First time Eua genuinely vulnerable/unguarded with Rufus
- Rufus stays present without trying to fix or manage her grief
- Establishes her father as reason she didn't become monster
- Shows what makes Eua trust enough to break down in someone's presence

### "Seventh Heaven Delivery"

### "Breaking Point" - Reno's Intervention
**Timeline:** During gala isolation period (mid-marriage)  
**Setting:** Charity gala → car → overlook on city's eastern edge

At gala where everyone avoids Eua due to fear post-proposition incident. She signals Reno with "headache" line. Breaks down in car—feels unwanted everywhere (Vincent, high society, friends). Worse than Shinra rubble because she chose this. Reno takes her to overlook instead of home. She vents: was functional facing actual danger but fancy party isolation breaks her. Reno reframes: "Not fragile—running out of ways to pretend you're fine. This is slow erosion." Encourages talking to Rufus: "You're allowed to not be okay."

**Key Development:**
- Isolation genuinely harms Eua where survival danger didn't
- Reno provides support without agenda, recognizes legitimate struggle
- First person to reframe her breakdown as reasonable response not weakness
- Sets up "Kneading Through It" conversation with Rufus

### "Kneading Through It"
**Timeline:** Same evening as Breaking Point  
**Setting:** Penthouse kitchen, late evening

Eua making bread aggressively when Rufus gets home. Explains everyone avoids her now but: "I don't blame you." If she were different, might've found his response romantic. "But I'm not that kind of woman. So—what the fuck do I do now, Rufus?" He offers three options: lean into fear, sidestep to smaller events with different people, or minimal attendance only. She rejects isolation: "I like people. That's what got me through Edge. Choosing not to because these people are assholes? That's just lonely." Interested in option two but feels like defeat. He reframes, leaves decision to her, offers to send info on smaller company initiatives.

**Key Development:**
- Eua's social needs are survival-level requirement not preference
- Rufus gives genuine options without pushing outcome
- He reduces her mandatory obligations without being asked
- Establishes her choosing smaller authentic events over prestigious isolation

### "The Quack"

Eua tracking conspiracy theorists, finds Dr. Marcus Richards—former Project Mother scientist, genetic stability analysis. Now posts accurate science on 300-view channel, systematically discredited by Shinra PR. She realizes her interview reinforced his "quack" reputation since everyone thinks she was joking. ## Character Insights

### Eua's Social Needs Are Survival-Level (CRITICAL)
Not preference—fundamentally requires human connection and conversation to thrive. Fancy party isolation genuinely harms her worse than survival danger or cult hunting. Will choose authentic interaction over prestigious positioning. Used social interaction as survival tool in Edge, needs it for wellbeing not just entertainment.

### Rufus's Learning Pattern
Overanalyzes behavior for tactical purpose when simple explanations exist (alcohol = strategy vs. just tastes bad). Can adjust expectations without defensiveness when corrected. Beginning to offer practical kindness without expecting return (Dr. Richards subscription, reducing events, stocking non-alcoholic options). Allows Eua autonomy in decisions affecting her directly.

### The Stalemate Dynamic
Both waiting for other to be vulnerable first, neither willing to take that risk. Eua trusts Rufus physically (believes he won't hurt her) but not emotionally (won't trust him with authentic self). Rufus admits he wouldn't know how to be vulnerable even if willing. Both honest about impasse existing rather than pretending it doesn't. This is foundation their relationship builds from.

### Eua's Relationship with Her Origins
Matter-of-fact, no bitterness about being lab-grown experiment. Can deploy truth as strategic tool when useful (viral interview). Feels empathy for others damaged by same system (Dr. Richards, Cloud). Father's humanity was antidote to being treated as property—reason she's not Sephiroth 2.0.

### Progress Indicators in Marriage
Small moments of genuine emotion witnessed (father's file grief, breakdown in car). Rufus reducing obligations/expectations without being asked. Eua asking for help rather than only deflecting or performing independence. Both choosing small kindnesses when large solutions impossible (anonymous support, event reduction, practical accommodations).
